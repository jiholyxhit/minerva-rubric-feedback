# #purpose

## Rubric definition

Articulate a mission statement that coherently connects fundamental values with long and short term goals, as well as the operating procedures that advance these goals in light of the values.

## Strong example — body application

The purpose of the non-profit organization that you want to volunteer in is "to create a radically inclusive, just and loving community, mobilized to alleviate suffering and break the cycles of poverty and marginalization." As you interrogate the organization and talk to its founders, you learn that one of their core values is the notion that all people deserve to be respected and empowered equally ('radically inclusive'), as well as a commitment to kind and pleasant interactions ('loving community'). Another core value is an egalitarian conception of social justice, according to which the way to respect and empower everyone equally is to ensure there are social conditions in place that prevent people from succumbing to poverty ('breaking the cycle of poverty and marginalization'). These values underlie their monthly goal of serving at least 60,000 meals to those who may otherwise go hungry. Likewise, their operating procedures reflect these values and goals as they give out free meals daily to anyone who comes to their walk-in center.

## Strong example — contextualization footnote

The organization's purpose was identified and further elaborated through the discussion of their values, goals, and operating procedures. To evaluate the organization's operation the tight connection between the mission's components is emphasized.

## Why this pair works

- **All four components are present and named.** Values ("radically inclusive," "loving community," egalitarian justice), long-term goal (break cycles of poverty), short-term goal (60,000 meals/month), and operating procedure (free daily walk-in meals) are all identified.
- **Connection between components is explicit.** The values are unpacked word-by-word from the mission statement ("radically inclusive" → everyone deserves equal empowerment), then shown to directly produce the goals, which in turn drive the procedures.
- **Procedures flow from both values AND goals.** The walk-in policy isn't just efficient — it reflects "loving community" (no barriers, anyone can come) and advances the monthly meal target.
- **The footnote signals the analytic move.** It identifies that the key work is tracing the *connections* between components, not just listing them — "the tight connection between mission components" is the evaluative lens.

## Evaluation criteria

### Strong body application
- Identifies all relevant components: core values, long-term goals, short-term goals, and operating procedures (or as many as the context makes available)
- Shows how each component follows from or is shaped by the others — values inform goals, goals drive procedures, procedures reflect values
- Goes beyond listing: the analysis should demonstrate that the mission is coherent (components fit together) or identify tensions where they do not
- Uses specific language from or about the mission statement, not generic description

### Strong footnote contextualization
- Names the specific values, goals, and/or procedures the student focused on
- Explains the *connection* between them — not just that they're related but how and why each flows from or supports the others
- Identifies what the coherence analysis reveals about the organization (or what tension it surfaces, if the components don't fit)
- Goes beyond restating the rubric ("I articulated the mission by connecting values, goals, and procedures")

## Common weaknesses — flag these when you see them

| Weakness | What it looks like | Stronger version |
|---|---|---|
| Components listed but not connected | "Their values are X. Their goals are Y. Their procedures are Z." | "Their value of radical inclusion directly produces the no-barrier walk-in policy, because turning anyone away would contradict the core commitment." |
| Mission restated, not analyzed | Paraphrasing the mission statement without interrogating how components relate | Unpack each word or phrase and show which value it embeds, then trace it forward to a goal or procedure |
| Only one component analyzed | Deep dive on values alone, with goals and procedures mentioned only in passing | All four components need to be present and connected |
| Coherence assumed, not argued | "The mission statement reflects their values and goals." | Show the specific link: which value → which goal → which procedure, and why that chain is tight rather than coincidental |
| Footnote describes, doesn't analyze | "The organization has a mission statement with values, goals, and procedures." | "I emphasized the tight connection between 'radical inclusion' (value) and the open walk-in policy (procedure) because that link shows the mission isn't aspirational rhetoric — it shapes daily operations." |
| Tension ignored when present | Presents the mission as fully coherent when components actually conflict | If values and procedures are misaligned, flag it — that's part of the analysis |
